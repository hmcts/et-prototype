Order a witness to attend and give evidence

Use this form to apply to order a witness to attend and give evidence.

You must explain to the tribunal why it is in the interests of justice to order this witness to attend.

The tribunal can limit the number of witnesses to be called to give evidence on a particular issue, especially if that issue is not central to the case.

If you call a witness to give evidence you will not normally be allowed to cross-examine them. This means you will not be able to prompt, challenge or contradict their evidence, or to ask them leading questions.

You must consider whether the evidence of this witness is likely to help your case.

You will be able to review your application before you submit it.
